# Fraud_Sense
FraudSense, an intelligent fraud detection system designed to identify suspicious job postings and fraudulent reviews using data-driven techniques.
The rapid expansion of digital platforms has transformed the way people search for employment
opportunities, purchase products, and share their experiences online. Job portals, recruitment websites, and
online review platforms have become essential tools for connecting individuals with opportunities and
information. While these platforms provide significant convenience and accessibility, they have also
created new opportunities for fraudulent activities. Fake job postings and deceptive online reviews have
become increasingly common, posing serious risks to users who rely on online information to make
important decisions. These fraudulent activities can mislead job seekers, manipulate consumer opinions,
and undermine the credibility of online platforms. Fake job postings are often created by malicious actors
who attempt to attract job seekers with unrealistic offers, attractive salaries, or misleading job descriptions.
In many cases, these postings are used to collect sensitive personal information or demand payment for
non-existent opportunities. Similarly, fraudulent online reviews are used to manipulate public perception of
products, services, or organizations. Such reviews may falsely promote certain services or unfairly damage
the reputation of legitimate businesses. Because of the large volume of content generated on digital
platforms every day, detecting these fraudulent activities manually becomes extremely challenging and
time-consuming. Traditional approaches to fraud detection often rely on manual verification, rule-based
filtering, or user reporting mechanisms. Although these methods can identify some suspicious activities,
they are often limited in their ability to detect complex patterns of fraudulent behavior. Fraudulent content
may be designed to appear legitimate, making it difficult for manual inspection or simple filtering rules to
identify it effectively. As a result, there is an increasing need for intelligent systems that can automatically
analyze large volumes of online data and detect patterns associated with fraudulent behavior.
Advancements in data science, machine learning, and natural language processing have made it possible to
develop systems capable of analyzing textual content and identifying hidden patterns within large datasets.
These technologies enable automated analysis of job postings and online reviews by examining linguistic
patterns, contextual information, and associated metadata. By learning from historical data containing both
legitimate and fraudulent examples, machine learning models can identify subtle characteristics that
distinguish genuine content from suspicious activity. This project presents FraudSense, a machine
learning-based fraud detection system designed to identify fraudulent job postings and deceptive online
reviews. The system analyzes textual data and related attributes using natural language processing
techniques and predictive models to detect suspicious patterns. By integrating machine learning models
with an application interface, the system provides users with an automated tool for evaluating the
authenticity of job postings and reviews. The goal of this project is to demonstrate how intelligent
analytical techniques can enhance online trust and help protect users from fraudulent digital content.
